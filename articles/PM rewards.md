**How to farm polymarket's $1M/day without predicting anything**
Source: https://x.com/i/article/2063997996723736624

polymarket does ~$1M in fees every day
none of that requires you to have a correct opinion about anything
here's how a smaller group of people pulls yield out of the order book no matter what actually happens
The insight that breaks your brain
 
the goal of LP farming is to place an order that never gets filled
you're not providing liquidity to help the market. you're sitting in the order book collecting rewards for resting near the midpoint, hoping nobody takes your trade
polymarket pays makers (anyone posting limit orders) based on how long their orders sat close to the midpoint. you get paid for being there, not for trading
How to set it up
 
find a market resolving in 21–24 hours. not 3 days, not 2 hours, that window specifically. far enough out for low volatility, close enough that rewards per hour are concentrated
place orders on both sides within 2% of the midpoint. tighter means more rewards. go too tight and someone fills you, and now you're gambling on an outcome instead of farming
the 10-cent rule: if either side drops below $0.10, you have to post on both sides to qualify. stops people from only sitting on the safe side of lopsided markets
spread across 50–100 small positions instead of one big one. a single filled order in a volatile market can wipe what took days to build
The FIFO problem
 
polymarket's book is first-in-first-out. whoever posted first gets filled first
if your order is at the front of the queue and the price moves, a trader picks you off before you can cancel. the serious farmers run bots that constantly repost orders, bumping themselves to the back of the queue on purpose so they're always last in line to get hit
do it manually without that and you're just exit liquidity
The actual risk
 
getting filled
if someone takes your order, you're suddenly holding a position in a prediction market right before it resolves. the no-risk farm just turned into a directional bet
the playbook: cancel everything 6–10 hours before the event. stepping away from the screen near resolution, close first. and if you do get filled, get out immediately even at a small loss. eating a few cents beats holding a coin flip into resolution
The airdrop layer
 
polymarket is doing ~$1M/day in fees. fully doxxed team. no token yet
LP activity is exactly the kind of on-chain behavior that tends to count in a snapshot. farming rewards now is probably farming eligibility at the same time
one caveat: wallets that only farm tend to get discounted. throw in some real trades on markets you actually have a read on, and the wallet stops looking like a bot
The meta
 
you can learn the whole thing in an afternoon. the reason it still works is that everyone's busy trying to pick winners, and almost nobody wants to just sit in the book collecting a yield that ignores the outcome entirely
one resting order pays you three ways at once: LP rewards, maker rebates, and a shot at the airdrop. that combination is rare
